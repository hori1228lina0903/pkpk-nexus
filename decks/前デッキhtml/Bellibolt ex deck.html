Deck Features

  • Writhe down the opponent with Darkrai ex's Ability by dealing a continuous 20 damage before finishing them off with Bellibolt ex's high-output 130 damage.
  • Enhance durability for long-term battles by utilizing Primordial Plains and Indeedee ex for HP recovery, while using Rocky Helmet to reflect damage back at high-power attackers.
  • While it is a slower, control-oriented build vulnerable to ultra-fast aggro, its strength lies in its stability and ability to gain an advantage against a wide range of ex decks.

Key Card Analysis

Bellibolt ex
  • The deck's primary attacker.
  • Delivers a powerful 140 damage with "High Voltage Cannon" when there are 4 or more Lightning Energy on your field.
  • Boosted by Training Court, it reaches 150 damage, allowing it to one-shot most opponent ex cards.
Tadbulb
  • The pre-evolution of Bellibolt ex.
  • Can be reliably searched with Poké Ball to support a stable setup.
  • An essential Basic Pokémon for early deployment to increase the deck's overall speed.
Zeraora
  • Functions as a sub-attacker and energy accelerator.
  • Assists in fulfilling Bellibolt ex’s 140-damage requirement by accelerating Lightning Energy through its Ability.
Electric Generator
  • A powerful energy acceleration Item.
  • Allows you to attach Lightning Energy to your Benched Pokémon on a successful coin flip.
  • A key card for satisfying Bellibolt ex's requirement of 4 Energy on the field.
Electric Cord
  • An Energy recovery Item.
  • Prevents Lightning Energy from being wasted in the Trash.
  • Provides vital support to maintain the high energy count required by Bellibolt ex.

How to Play Bellibolt ex Deck

  1. Deploy Tadbulb to the Active Spot and use Electric Generator and Electric Cord to quickly accelerate 4 or more Lightning Energy onto your field.
  2. Set up Zeraora on the Bench to use its Ability for additional energy acceleration, supporting the setup for Bellibolt ex while serving as a reliable sub-attacker.
  3. Evolve Tadbulb into Bellibolt ex and unleash consecutive "High Voltage Cannon" attacks for 140 damage to one-shot opponent ex cards.
  4. Utilize Oricorio as a defensive wall against ex decks; move it to the Active Spot when the opponent deploys their ex Pokémon to nullify attacks and buy time for your team.
  5. If Bellibolt ex is knocked out, switch to Zeraora to maintain pressure while re-accelerating energy and preparing your next Bellibolt ex for a continuous offensive.
