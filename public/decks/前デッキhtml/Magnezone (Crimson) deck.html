Deck Features

  • A "No Pokémon ex" deck, consisting entirely of non-ex cards.
  • Utilize Oricorio as a wall to maintain early-game pressure while using Magnezone's high damage output to take down the opponent's main cards.
  • Use Magneton's Ability to gather two or more Energy cards before evolving into Magnezone and moving it to the Active Spot.
  • In addition to being a wall, Oricorio is deployed to the Active Spot when the opponent plays a Pokémon ex to nullify their attacks.
  • Since the deck lacks overall bulk, it is at a disadvantage against the opponent's high-damage decks.

Key Card Analysis

Magnezone
  • Deals 90 damage with an attack while forcing a coin toss.
  • Has high HP for a Lightning type, providing good durability on the field.
Magnezone
  • Main attacker.
  • Possesses a high damage output of 110 for 3 Energy.
Magneton
  • An Energy accelerator that can attach one Lightning Energy to itself every turn via its Ability.
  • Should be evolved into Magnezone after attaching 2 or more Lightning Energy.
Oricorio
  • Wall and attacker.
  • Capable of completely shutting down the opponent if their Pokémon is an ex card.
Clemont
  • Search card for the evolution line.
  • Accelerates the setup leading to the main attackers.
  • A one-copy inclusion as it loses its purpose in the late game.

How to Play Magnezone Deck

  1. Deploy Oricorio to the Active Spot to effectively nullify attacks from the opponent's Pokémon ex.
  2. Evolve Magneton on the Bench and use its Ability to attach Lightning Energy from your deck.
  3. Once Magneton has 2 to 3 Lightning Energy attached, evolve it into Magnezone.
  4. Consistently deal 110 damage with Magnezone every turn to knock out opponents in 1 or 2 hits.
  5. If a new Pokémon ex appears, switch Oricorio back to the Active Spot to block their attacks once again.
